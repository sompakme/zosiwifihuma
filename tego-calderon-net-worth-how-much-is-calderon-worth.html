Tego Calderon Biography

Tego Calderon, whose real name is Tegui Calderon Rosario, was born in Santurce, Puerto Rico in February 1972. He moved from Puerto Rico to Miami, Florida as a child and developed a passion for music. Calderon studied percussion and worked as a drummer in a rock band.

He was influenced by Puerto Rican and jazz music, eventually combining various genres in his own unique style.

Tego Calderon Career

Tego Calderon’s career took off with the release of his debut album, “El Abayarde,” in 2002. This marked his entry into the music industry as one of the pioneers of reggaeton, a genre that combines elements of Latin and Caribbean music with hip-hop and dancehall.

Calderon quickly gained recognition for his unique sound and powerful lyrics, which reflected his experiences growing up in Puerto Rico and Miami. His music resonated with audiences worldwide, and he became known for his energetic performances and charismatic stage presence.

One of the defining moments in Calderon’s career came when he became the first rap artist to perform at the Puerto Rican National Day of Salsa. This groundbreaking performance showcased his versatility as an artist and solidified his status as a trailblazer in the music industry.
