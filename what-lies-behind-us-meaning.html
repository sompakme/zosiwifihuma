For what lies behind us?

“What lies behind us, and what lies before us are but tiny matters compared to what lies within us.” —Ralph Waldo Emerson | PassItOn.com.

What is the meaning of this quote what lies behind us and what lies before us are small matters compared to what lies within us?

Ralph Waldo Emerson has truly quoted “what lies behind us, and what lies before us, are tiny matters compared to what lies within us.” The elements within us are strong enough to make our lives more clear and positive towards our goals.

What happens when you tell a lie?

When we engage in deceit, our respiratory and heart rates increase, we start to sweat, our mouth goes dry, and our voice can shake. Some of these physiological effects form the basis of the classic lie-detector (polygraph) test. People vary in their ability to tell a lie due, in part, to differences in the brain.
