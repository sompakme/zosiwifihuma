Salary Payment Details

Faculty and staff at the University of Missouri receive their salaries through a structured payment schedule. The payment frequency varies depending on the type of employment, with exempt or salaried employees receiving monthly pay and non-exempt or hourly employees receiving bi-weekly pay.

Direct Deposit:

All salary payments are made through direct deposit, ensuring convenient and secure transactions. Employees can designate their preferred bank account for direct deposit, eliminating the need for physical checks.

Pay Stubs:

To access pay-related information, employees can conveniently access their pay stubs through the myHR system. This allows for easy tracking and reference of payment details, including earnings, deductions, and tax withholdings.

Payment Schedule:

The payment schedule for monthly and bi-weekly employees at the University of Missouri follows specific timelines:

  • Monthly paid positions are disbursed on the last regularly-scheduled workday of each month.
  • Hourly paid positions are paid 11 calendar days after the end of the pay period.

It is important for employees to be aware of their payment schedule and plan their finances accordingly. The reliability and efficiency of the payment system ensure that employees receive their salaries in a timely manner.

Special Tax Considerations for International Employees

International employees at the University of Missouri may have special tax considerations based on their visa or resident status. To ensure compliance with tax regulations, the university provides resources and guidance for both employees and employers.

It is essential for campus departments and off-campus employers to be aware of these tax considerations when processing paperwork for international employees. By understanding and addressing these tax implications, both employees and employers can avoid potential issues and ensure proper tax reporting and withholding.

Visa and Resident Status

The tax considerations for international employees largely depend on their visa or resident status. Different visa categories may have specific requirements and limitations regarding employment and tax obligations. It is important for international employees to consult with immigration and tax professionals to understand the implications of their specific visa or resident status.

Tax Treaties

Tax treaties between the United States and other countries may also impact international employees’ tax liabilities. These treaties aim to prevent double taxation and provide benefits to eligible individuals. International employees should review the tax treaty provisions applicable to their home country to understand if any tax benefits or exemptions apply.

Reporting Requirements

International employees may be required to file additional tax forms and documentation, such as the Form 1040NR or Form 1040NR-EZ. These forms are specifically designed for nonresident aliens and should be filed in addition to the regular tax return, Form 1040. It is crucial for international employees to stay informed about the reporting requirements applicable to their situation.

Foreign Earned Income Exclusion

The foreign earned income exclusion allows qualifying individuals to exclude a portion of their foreign income from U.S. taxation. International employees who meet the requirements may be eligible for this exclusion. However, it is important to note that claiming this exclusion may have implications on other tax benefits, such as the eligibility for certain tax credits.
