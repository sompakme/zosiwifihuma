Smith & Wesson Revolvers

The Smith & Wesson Revolvers section of the forum is a valuable resource for enthusiasts interested in S&W revolvers. Whether you’re fascinated by Smith & Wesson Antiques, S&W Lever Action Pistols, or Tip-Up Revolvers, this section provides a dedicated space for discussions and insights.

Enthusiasts can join various sub-forums within this section to explore different categories and models of Smith & Wesson revolvers. One of the notable categories is S&W Antiques, where you can delve into the rich history of Smith & Wesson firearms and engage in discussions about collector items.

Another popular sub-forum is dedicated to S&W Hand Ejectors, spanning from 1896 to 1961. This model line holds significant historical value and captures the essence of Smith & Wesson’s revolver craftsmanship.

For those interested in collecting newer Smith & Wesson revolvers, there are discussions around the revolvers produced from 1961 to the present. From classic designs to modern innovations, users can find extensive tips, maintenance guides, and insights on various S&W revolver models in this section.

Join the Smith & Wesson Revolvers section to connect with fellow enthusiasts, expand your knowledge on Smith & Wesson Antiques, Hand Ejectors, and other models, and indulge in the rich history and craftsmanship of these iconic firearms.

Key Disciplines

Within the Smith & Wesson Competitive Shooting section, you’ll find discussions related to various shooting disciplines:

  • IPSC (International Practical Shooting Confederation): A dynamic and fast-paced shooting sport that combines accuracy, speed, and movement.
  • IDPA (International Defensive Pistol Association): Focuses on realistic defensive scenarios, testing shooters’ skills in practical, self-defense-oriented courses of fire.
  • Silhouette Shooting: Involves shooting at steel targets in the shape of animals or geometric forms at various distances, testing accuracy and precision.
  • Bullseye Shooting: A precision-oriented discipline where shooters aim for small targets, testing their ability to consistently hit the center.

“Competitive shooting with Smith & Wesson firearms brings together a community of like-minded individuals who share a passion for both the sport and the brand. It’s not just about the competition; it’s also about the camaraderie and the continuous pursuit of excellence.” – Anonymous Competitive Shooter
