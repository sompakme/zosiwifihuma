Early Life and Art Career of David Choe

David Choe, a renowned American artist and graffiti pioneer, has a fascinating early life and art career that shaped his unique style and propelled him to international recognition.

David Choe was born on April 21, 1976, in Los Angeles, California. Growing up in the Koreatown area, he discovered his talent for drawing at a young age. Inspired by graffiti artists Hex and Mear One, Choe delved into the world of graffiti in 1990, experimenting with bold colors and intricate designs.

“Graffiti taught me how to deal with fear, how to be courageous, how to feel free, how to get up after you fall, and how to live in the present moment.”

Choe’s art career took an unexpected turn when his parents’ real estate business was burned down during the Los Angeles riots. This event led him to drop out of high school and embark on a two-year hitchhiking journey across America, Africa, Europe, and the Middle East. These travels exposed him to diverse cultures and experiences, shaping his artistic perspective.

Although he briefly attended the California College of the Arts, Choe decided to pursue his art career independently. He gained recognition for his graffiti work, showcasing his unique style that blended urban aesthetics with intricate details. Magazines like “Vice” took notice of his talent and commissioned him to create illustrations, giving him a platform to reach a wider audience.

David Choe’s artistic endeavors extended beyond graffiti and illustrations. He self-published the graphic novel “Slow Jams,” a collection of his unconventional and thought-provoking artwork. He also held solo exhibitions at prestigious museums and galleries worldwide, captivating audiences with his vivid and expressive creations.

Through his art, David Choe pushed boundaries and explored themes of sexuality, urban life, and social issues. His work challenged conventional norms and resonated with viewers, making a lasting impact on the art scene.

Facebook Payday – David Choe’s Fortunate Investment

In the early 2000s, the impressive graffiti artwork of David Choe captured the attention of Facebook President Sean Parker. Recognizing Choe’s talent, Parker hired him to decorate the walls of Facebook’s headquarters, an opportunity that would prove to be life-changing for the artist.

During the negotiation process, Choe made a fateful decision that would ultimately contribute to his immense wealth. Instead of opting for a traditional cash payment, he chose to receive $60,000 worth of company stock, thus becoming one of the early recipients of Facebook stock options.

The impact of this choice was monumental. After Facebook’s IPO, the value of its stock skyrocketed, propelling Choe’s equity from a seemingly modest sum to an astonishing figure. While the exact number of shares Choe received remains undisclosed, estimates suggest his Facebook stock was worth approximately $200 million at the time of the IPO.

As Facebook continued to flourish in the years that followed, Choe’s holdings experienced significant growth. Presently, it is believed that his stock options could be valued between $300-500 million, solidifying his status as one of the wealthiest figures in the art world.

Choe’s collaboration with Facebook not only brought immense financial success but also established a lasting relationship with Sean Parker, further fueling his notable career. His decorative murals continue to adorn the walls of Facebook’s headquarters and serve as a testament to the fruitful partnership between Choe and the social media giant.
