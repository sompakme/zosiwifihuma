Born on the 21st of July, 1986, Elizabeth Folan Gilpin grew up in the South Street Seaport neighborhood of Manhattan. She is the daughter of Jack Gilpin and Ann McDonough. Both of her parents are actors as well.

Betty Gilpin is a graduate of Fordham University with a Bachelor of Arts degree in 2008 and studied under Dianne Wiest during her time at Fordham. Prior to this, in 2004 she attended The Loomis Chaffee School.

Starring roles in numerous television series, including ‘Law & Order: Criminal Intent’, ‘Fringe, Medium’, ‘Law & Order: Special Victims Unit’, and ‘Elementary’, Gilpin began her acting career. Her off-Broadway in productions such as ‘Heartless’, ‘I’m Gonna Pray for You So Hard’, and ‘We Live Here’ are also well renowned.

Her supporting roles in such films as ‘Ghost Town’ (2008), ‘Take Care’ (2014), ‘True Story’ (2015), and ‘Future ’38’ (2017) also gave her a part of her fame. Inspired by the 1980s female professional wrestling league of the same name the Netflix comedy series GLOW (2017) gave Betty the breakthrough she needed with her role starring as Debbie “Liberty Belle” Eagan.

In 2019 Gilpin appeared in the comedy-drama film ‘A Dog’s Journey’, the action comedy film ‘Stuber’, the fantasy romantic comedy film ‘Isn’t It Romantic’, and the action-horror film ‘The Hunt’. She has also been nominated for three Gold Derby Awards (2018), Critics’ Choice Television Awards (2018, 2019), Screen Actors Guild Awards (2018, 2019) and Primetime Emmy Awards (2018, 2019).

She met movie star named Cosmos Colyer Pfeil in 2015, they decided to get married and tied a knot on 6 August 2016.
