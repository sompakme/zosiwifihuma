Inside The Real Estate Portfolio Of Tennis Champion Novak Djokovic

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, September 10, 2024

  • Two SoHo Apartments
  • Monte Carlo Residence

With a career filled with tennis victories, Novak Djokovic is one of the best tennis players in the world, and the star has heavily invested in real estate from his sprawling $220 million fortune.

Novak Djokovic began playing tennis at just four years of age and caught the eye of Jelena Genčić, who trained him after realizing his potential. After six years, young Djokovic moved to Germany to train under Nikola Pilić and participated in junior tournaments. After turning pro in 2003, it was a slow and steady rise for Djokovic, who eventually won his first major Grand Slam in 2008 at the Australian Open. Since then, he has won 21 Grand Slam titles and collected $150 million in prize money, the most amount for any player in tennis history. Through his brand endorsements and partnership, Djokovic makes additional millions.

With a net worth of over $220 million, Novak Djokovic has become one of the wealthiest tennis players and is considered one of the greatest of all time. His hefty net worth allows him to spend on expensive cars and family vacations. The tennis player has been known to buy many real estate properties since winning his first Grand Slam. Let us take a look at the real estate legacy of Novak Djokovic.

Childhood Home

A Serbian native, Novak Djokovic comes from a humble background in Serbia, where he lived with his parents in Belgrade. According to Hello Magazine, his grandparents resided in a modest concrete building block with traditional flats. The window of his apartment overlooked a parking lot for the residents. A football park where he often played with his friends was adjoining the building. He lived close to now-retired Serbian football player Neven Markovic.

Two SoHo Apartments

Moving to his real estate in the United States, Novak Djokovic paid $10 million to buy two apartments in the expensive SoHo neighborhood in New York City. Famous architect Renzo Piano designed the interior of the building, and each of his condos features two bedrooms, as noted by Architectural Digest. The apartments he purchased are not located on a single floor, meaning they were not combined into a single unit. The 30-story apartment finished its construction in 2018, and the two towers with 115 units opened for residents.

Monte Carlo Residence

The star athlete first visited Monte Carlo when he was 17 years old and trained with his couch for a short period. He began spending significant time in the city due to his tournaments and the coach’s residence in the area. His house is on a hillside property that offers stunning Mediterranean sea views. As the city is home to many tennis athletes and sports professionals, it allows Djokovic easier access to tennis centers for practice and is spotted at the Monte Carlo Country Club.

Marbella Home

While Novak Djokovic made Monte Carlo his primary residence for over a decade, he moved with his young family to Marbella in 2020. He spent the initial months of the pandemic with his family at the $10 million mansion. The home has a Moroccan-style interior with nine bedrooms and eight bathrooms, as stated by Cheat Sheet. The luxurious home is often seen on Instagram, and Djokovic has been snapped practicing tennis with his children on the tennis court. Along with offering scenic views of the mountain and sea, the Marbella home has multiple jacuzzies, a sauna, a Turkish bath, and a private spa.

Miami Penthouse

Every year Novak Djokovic plays the Miami Open, and the athlete often takes a rest at his Miami penthouse at Eighty-Seven Park. The penthouse is another famous creation by Renzo Piano and features three bedrooms. As mentioned by GQ, the penthouse is equipped with various amenities, including stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and Miami Parkland. The building has an infinity pool, valet-on-service, spa, gym, library, and a botanical bar. The expensive apartment is located on one of the most exclusive streets of Miami and is home to many celebrities and businesspeople.

Belgrade Apartment

A Belgrade native, Novak Djokovic decided to buy an apartment in his hometown in 2018. The tennis star purchased a penthouse for $675,000 that overlooks Lake Pavlova. It is located in one of the most expensive buildings in the city and features three bedrooms. Additional rooms in the home include a kitchen, a large family room, and three bathrooms. The building has a terrace pool for Djokovic and his family to enjoy. It is also reported that he purchased another apartment that he gave to his friend Filip Krajinovic.

From penthouses in the United States to cozy homes in Europe, Novak Djokovic has a substantial real estate portfolio that spans cities vital to him. While many of the houses are located in locations for tournaments, other homes relate to his country of Serbia. As the tennis star looks for more avenues to invest, he will soon be seen buying more properties for his young family.
